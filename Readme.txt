It is the year 1612 of the FOURTH AGE. A new land has been discovered, far across the great Ocean. Here, great civilizations collide 


Attributes:
All attributes are measured from a scale of 1 - 20, 1 being absolute bottom, 4 being inferior, 6 being subpar, 9 being average, 12 being superior, 15 being excellent, and 20 being superhuman

	Strength - measures the physical strength of a character, STR 1 characters are weaker than a baby, and STR 20 could defeat Hercules with no effort.
	Dexterity - measures the physical agililty of a character, AGI 1 characters are bulls in a China shop, and AGI 20 can dodge a speeding bullet and outrun a cheetah.
	Constitution - measures the physical endurance and health of a character, CON 1 characters are constantly on death's door, and CON 20 characters can run a Spartathlon without breaking a sweat
	Intelligence - measures the mental ability and logical reasoning of a character, INT 1 characters are complete morons, and INT 20 characters are human computers.
	Wisdom - measures the common sense and insight a character has, WIS 1 characters have no sense whatesover, and WIS 20 characters are wise far beyond their years.
	Charisma - measures the character's ability to attract and communicate, CHA 1 characters are probably friendless losers lmao, and CHA 20 characters can charm the Devil himself.

Skills:
	Combat: Skills that affect fighting
		One-Handed: The ability to fight well with any one-handed weapon, from daggers to swords, clubs to maces, and hatchets to axes.
		Two-Handed: The ability to fight well with weapons requiring two hands, typically larger swords, blunt weapons, and axes.
		Polearms: The ability to fight well with spears, halberds, and pikes.
		Archery: The ability to fight well with bows and arrows.
		Crossbows: The ability to fight well with crossbows.
		Throwing: The ability to fight well with thrown weapons, from rocks to javelins and grenades.
		Firearms: The ability to fight well with arquebuses, muskets, pistols, blunderbusses, and any other gunpowder-based weapon.
	Magic: Skills that involve the magical arts
		Alchemy: The manipulation of chemical compounds to change their properties
		Conjuration: Manipulation of spirits, to bring them into this world or cast them out.
		Elemental: Manipulation of the Six Elements: Earth, Fire, Water, Air, Electromagnetism, and Force
		Natural: Manipulation of plants, animals and the animistic spirits, 
		Necromancy:  Manipulation of life and death to reanimate corpses, call souls from the dead, and 
		Thaumaturgy: Manipulation of miracles to create, restore, and strengthen life
